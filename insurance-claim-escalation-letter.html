Insurance Claim Escalation Letter

What This Situation Means

Claim escalation involves requesting supervisory or management review of unresolved claim issues after initial adjuster communications have failed to resolve the matter. Escalation is appropriate when delays persist, responses are inadequate, or disputes remain unresolved after reasonable attempts at resolution.

Most insurance companies have internal escalation procedures allowing policyholders to request higher-level review. Escalation should be used after standard claim handling processes have been exhausted, not as a first response.

Why Insurers Do This

Escalation may be needed due to:

  • Unresponsive or non-communicative adjusters
  • Persistent delays without justification
  • Disagreements that cannot be resolved at adjuster level
  • Procedural errors or policy misinterpretation
  • Lack of authority at adjuster level to resolve issues
  • Need for management oversight of claim handling

⚠️ What NOT to Do

  • Do not escalate without first attempting resolution with assigned adjuster
  • Do not threaten litigation or regulatory complaints without attorney consultation
  • Do not escalate without documenting previous communication attempts
  • Do not use escalation as first contact method
  • Do not send hostile or emotional escalation letters

What to Do Next

Document all previous communication attempts with the assigned adjuster. Create timeline showing delays or unresolved issues. Request supervisor or manager contact information from the insurance company.

Submit formal escalation correspondence outlining specific issues, previous attempts at resolution, and requested action. Maintain professional tone focusing on factual issues. If escalation fails to resolve issues, consider state insurance department complaint or attorney consultation.
