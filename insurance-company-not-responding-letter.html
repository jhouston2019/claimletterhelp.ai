Insurance Company Not Responding Letter

What This Situation Means

When an insurance company fails to respond to claim submissions, status inquiries, or other correspondence, it may violate state insurance regulations requiring timely acknowledgment and communication. Most states mandate specific response timeframes for initial claim acknowledgment and ongoing status updates.

Complete lack of response is different from processing delays. No response may indicate administrative problems, lost documentation, or intentional non-communication tactics.

Why Insurers Do This

Common reasons for non-response include:

  • Claim never received or lost in processing
  • Contact information incorrect or outdated
  • Internal routing or assignment failures
  • Adjuster turnover or vacancy
  • System errors or administrative backlogs
  • Intentional delay or non-response tactics

⚠️ What NOT to Do

  • Do not assume your claim was received without confirmation
  • Do not continue waiting indefinitely without escalation
  • Do not send hostile or threatening communications
  • Do not file duplicate claims unless advised by the insurer
  • Do not miss policy deadlines while waiting for insurer response

What to Do Next

Document all attempts to contact the insurer, including dates, methods, and results. Verify your contact information is correct. Send certified mail correspondence documenting your claim submission and lack of response.

If no response continues beyond state-mandated timeframes, file a complaint with your state insurance department. For persistent non-communication involving significant claims, consult an attorney. For initial written demands for communication, you may prepare correspondence using this system.
