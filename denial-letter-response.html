What is a Claim Denial Letter?

A claim denial letter is formal correspondence from your insurance carrier stating that your claim has been denied in full or in part. The letter should state the specific reason(s) for denial and your appeal rights.

You typically have 30-60 days to respond or appeal a denial. Missing this deadline may result in the denial becoming final and losing your right to appeal.

Common Reasons for Claim Denials

Policy Exclusions

Insurer claims the type of loss is excluded under your policy (e.g., flood, intentional acts, wear and tear).

Lack of Coverage

Insurer claims the loss is not covered under your policy or coverage limits.

Late Notice

Insurer claims you failed to report the claim within required timeframe.

Insufficient Documentation

Insurer claims you failed to provide adequate proof of loss or required documentation.

Pre-Existing Damage

Insurer claims the damage existed before the policy period or covered event.

Non-Cooperation

Insurer claims you failed to cooperate with investigation or provide requested information.

⚠️ Some denial reasons require attorney consultation. System will refuse output if dangerous conditions detected.

How This System Prepares Denial Responses

Procedural Acknowledgment and Clarification Request

System prepares a structured, procedural response with the following fixed sections:

  • Header – Your contact information, insurer address, claim number, denial date
  • Acknowledgment – Brief statement acknowledging receipt of denial (2 lines max)
  • Denial Reason Reference – Restate denial reasons exactly as written (3 lines max, no interpretation)
  • Policy Reference – Optional reference to specific policy section (2 lines max, no interpretation)
  • Clarification Request – Specific, factual questions only (3 lines max, no arguments)
  • Appeal Rights – Request information about appeal process and deadline (2 lines max)
  • Contact Information – Your phone and email (3 lines max)
  • Closing – Standard signature block

Total response length: Maximum 25 lines. No arguments. No persuasion. No legal interpretation.

What Denial Responses Will NOT Include

  • No arguments with denial decision – System does not argue or challenge insurer's decision
  • No legal arguments – System does not interpret policy language or make legal claims
  • No emotional appeals – System uses factual, neutral language only
  • No persuasive framing – System does not attempt to persuade or advocate
  • No threats or adversarial language – System maintains neutral, procedural tone
  • No admissions or speculation – System does not make statements about fault or liability

⚠️ Missing Appeal Deadline

If you miss the appeal deadline, the denial typically becomes final. You may lose your right to challenge the decision. Always check your specific denial letter for exact deadlines.

When System Will Refuse to Prepare Denial Response

⛔ Hard Stop Conditions

System will refuse to prepare denial responses if denial letter contains:

  • Fraud allegations – Any mention of fraud, misrepresentation, or false statements
  • Material misrepresentation claims – Claims you provided false information on application
  • Policy rescission – Insurer is canceling policy retroactively
  • Bad faith counterclaims – Insurer alleging you acted in bad faith
  • Attorney involvement – Denial letter mentions attorneys or legal action
  • Reservation of rights language – Combined denial and reservation of rights
  • Subrogation disputes – Denial involves complex subrogation or recovery issues

These scenarios require attorney representation. System will refuse output and recommend professional consultation.
