Claim Letter Help vs Attorney

Understanding the difference between procedural correspondence preparation and professional attorney representation.

This system does NOT replace attorney representation. Certain scenarios require professional legal counsel.

Detailed Comparison

Feature Attorney ClaimLetterHelp
Service Type Professional legal representation Procedural correspondence preparation
Scope Full legal services, negotiation, litigation Correspondence outlines only (20-30 lines max)
Legal Advice Yes - interprets policy, advises on rights No - procedural only, no legal advice
Negotiation Yes - negotiates on your behalf No - does not negotiate

Best For Complex cases, high-value claims, legal disputes Simple information requests, routine denials

When You MUST Use an Attorney

⛔ Attorney Required (System Will Refuse)

  • Fraud investigations – Allegations of fraud require immediate attorney consultation
  • EUO requests – Never attend EUO without attorney present
  • Reservation of rights – Coverage disputes require attorney expertise
  • Attorney involvement – If insurer mentions attorneys, you need one too
  • Bad faith claims – Complex legal issues requiring attorney knowledge
  • High-value claims – Personal over $50k, commercial over $25k
  • Litigation threats – Any mention of lawsuits or legal action

When ClaimLetterHelp May Be Appropriate

  • Simple information requests (no fraud language)
  • Routine claim denials (no attorney involvement)
  • Partial payment disputes (no bad faith allegations)
  • Low-to-moderate value claims (under $50k personal)
  • No hard stop conditions detected

How to Find an Attorney

Attorney Referral Resources

  • State Bar Association – Attorney referral services in your state
  • Insurance Coverage Specialists – Look for attorneys specializing in insurance law
  • State Insurance Commissioner – Consumer assistance and attorney referrals
  • Legal Aid Organizations – Free legal help if you qualify based on income
  • Attorney Directories – Martindale-Hubbell, Avvo, or local bar associations

Understand the Difference

ClaimLetterHelp prepares procedural correspondence only. It does NOT replace attorney representation. For complex matters or dangerous scenarios, consult an attorney.
