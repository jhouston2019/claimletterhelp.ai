Insurance Claim Underpaid Letter

What This Situation Means

An underpaid claim occurs when the insurer's payment is less than the documented loss value or replacement cost. Underpayment differs from partial denial in that the insurer has not formally rejected portions of the claim, but simply paid insufficient amounts for covered items.

Underpayment may result from calculation errors, incomplete damage assessment, use of lower valuation methodologies, or failure to include all covered losses. Policyholders can dispute underpayments by providing documentation of actual costs and values.

Why Insurers Do This

Common underpayment causes include:

  • Mathematical or administrative calculation errors
  • Incomplete damage scope or missed items
  • Lower-than-market repair or replacement cost estimates
  • Excessive depreciation or betterment deductions
  • Failure to apply replacement cost coverage properly
  • Use of preferred vendor pricing instead of actual costs

⚠️ What NOT to Do

  • Do not deposit settlement checks if they include release language
  • Do not proceed with repairs if payment is insufficient to cover actual costs
  • Do not assume initial payment represents full claim settlement
  • Do not wait extended periods to dispute underpayment
  • Do not accept underpayment simply to close the claim

What to Do Next

Compare the payment amount to your documentation of actual loss value. Obtain contractor estimates or professional appraisals showing higher costs. Identify specific discrepancies between payment amount and documented values.

Submit written supplemental claim documentation with evidence of underpayment. Include receipts, estimates, or appraisals supporting higher values. For significant underpayments, consider hiring a public adjuster. For procedural underpayment disputes, you may prepare correspondence using this system.
