Insurance Payment Dispute Letter

What This Situation Means

A payment dispute involves disagreement about the calculation method, amount, or basis for an insurance claim payment. Disputes may concern depreciation formulas, replacement cost determinations, valuation methodologies, or application of deductibles and policy limits.

Payment disputes differ from denials in that coverage is not questioned, but rather the mathematical calculation or valuation approach used to determine payment amount. These disputes often require detailed documentation and comparison of calculation methods.

Why Insurers Do This

Common payment dispute issues include:

  • Different depreciation calculation methodologies
  • Disagreement about replacement cost vs. actual cash value
  • Use of different pricing databases or estimating systems
  • Disputes about betterment or upgrade deductions
  • Incorrect application of deductibles or policy limits
  • Mathematical calculation errors

⚠️ What NOT to Do

  • Do not accept disputed calculations without requesting explanation and documentation
  • Do not sign releases accepting payment calculations you dispute
  • Do not assume insurer calculation methods are non-negotiable
  • Do not dispute without alternative calculations or supporting documentation
  • Do not delay dispute while accepting partial payments with release language

What to Do Next

Request detailed breakdown of payment calculations from the insurer. Review your policy language regarding valuation methods and depreciation. Obtain independent calculations or appraisals using standard industry methods.

Submit written dispute identifying specific calculation errors or methodology disagreements. Include supporting documentation showing alternative calculations. For complex valuation disputes, consider hiring a public adjuster or appraiser. For procedural calculation disputes, you may prepare correspondence using this system.
