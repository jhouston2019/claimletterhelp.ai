What is a Reservation of Rights Letter?

A reservation of rights (ROR) letter is a formal notice from your insurance carrier stating that they are investigating your claim but "reserving their rights" to deny coverage later based on policy exclusions or conditions.

This means your insurance company is questioning whether your claim is covered under your policy. This is a serious legal issue that requires immediate attorney consultation.

What Reservation of Rights Means

Coverage is Being Questioned

The insurer is investigating whether your policy covers this specific claim. They may deny coverage entirely.

Policy Exclusions May Apply

The insurer believes certain policy exclusions or conditions may bar coverage for your claim.

Your Rights Are at Risk

How you respond can affect your coverage rights. Self-response is extremely dangerous.

Legal Complexity

Reservation of rights involves complex insurance law and policy interpretation requiring attorney expertise.

Common Reservation of Rights Scenarios

Policy Exclusion Questions

Insurer believes a policy exclusion may apply to your claim (e.g., intentional acts, business use, pre-existing damage).

Policy Condition Violations

Insurer claims you violated policy conditions (e.g., late notice, failure to cooperate, material misrepresentation).

Coverage Scope Disputes

Insurer questions whether the type of loss is covered under your policy (e.g., flood vs. water damage, business vs. personal use).

Third-Party Liability Issues

In liability claims, insurer questions whether you are legally liable or whether coverage applies to the specific incident.

All of these scenarios require attorney representation. Do not attempt to respond without legal counsel.
