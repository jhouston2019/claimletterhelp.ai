Insurance Written Appeal Letter

What This Document Is

A written appeal letter is formal documentation submitted to an insurance company requesting reconsideration of a denied or partially denied claim. Written appeals must follow specific procedures, include required documentation, and meet deadlines established in the policy or denial letter.

Written appeals are typically required before pursuing other remedies such as appraisal, mediation, or litigation. Appeals should address specific denial reasons with factual evidence and policy references, avoiding emotional arguments or irrelevant information.

What Should Be Included

Written appeals should contain:

  • Policy number and claim number identification
  • Clear statement of appeal request
  • Specific denial reasons being challenged
  • Factual evidence addressing each denial reason
  • Supporting documentation and references to policy language
  • Professional, non-emotional tone throughout

⚠️ Appeal Letter Mistakes to Avoid

  • Missing appeal deadlines specified in denial letter
  • Failing to address specific denial reasons
  • Including emotional appeals instead of factual evidence
  • Submitting appeals without supporting documentation
  • Attempting to appeal fraud allegations or legal matters without attorney

How to Prepare Written Appeals

Review denial letter carefully to identify specific reasons and appeal deadlines. Gather all documentation that addresses denial reasons, including estimates, receipts, photos, and professional assessments. Draft appeal letter addressing each denial reason with factual responses and supporting evidence.

Submit appeal via certified mail with return receipt. Maintain copies of all documents. If appeal involves complex legal issues or significant claim amounts, consult an attorney. For procedural appeals of straightforward denials, you may prepare documentation using this system.
