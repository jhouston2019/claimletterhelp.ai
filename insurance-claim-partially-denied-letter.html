Insurance Claim Partially Denied Letter

What This Situation Means

A partial denial occurs when an insurer approves some portions of your claim while denying others. The letter should specify which items or amounts are approved, which are denied, and the reasons for each denial. Partial denials are common in property claims with multiple damaged items or categories.

Partial denials may result from policy coverage limitations, disputes about causation for specific items, or disagreements about whether certain damage is included in the claim. Each denied item should have specific justification.

Why Insurers Do This

Common partial denial reasons include:

  • Some items covered, others fall under policy exclusions
  • Dispute about causation for specific damage
  • Some damage deemed pre-existing or unrelated
  • Coverage limits apply to certain categories
  • Insufficient documentation for specific items
  • Disagreement about scope of related damage

⚠️ What NOT to Do

  • Do not accept partial payment without reviewing denial reasons for other items
  • Do not sign releases that waive rights to appeal denied portions
  • Do not assume partial approval means denied items cannot be challenged
  • Do not delay appeal of denied portions while accepting payment for approved items
  • Do not fail to document why denied items should be covered

What to Do Next

Review the partial denial letter carefully to understand exactly which items were denied and why. Compare denial reasons to your policy language. Gather additional documentation or evidence supporting coverage for denied items.

You may accept payment for approved portions while appealing denied items, but review any release language carefully. If denied portions involve significant value or complex coverage disputes, consult an attorney. For procedural appeals of denied items, you may prepare correspondence using this system.
