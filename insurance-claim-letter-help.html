What is Insurance Claim Letter Help?

Insurance claim letter help is a procedural, risk-aware system that assists policyholders in preparing limited correspondence in response to letters from their insurance carriers. This is NOT legal advice, NOT negotiation, and NOT a replacement for professional representation.

When you receive a letter from your insurance company regarding a claim — whether it's a denial, a request for information, or a partial payment — you may need to respond within specific timeframes. This system helps prepare procedural correspondence outlines for certain types of letters, while refusing to generate output for dangerous scenarios.

What This System DOES

  • ✓ Classifies claim type and letter phase
  • ✓ Detects dangerous scenarios
  • ✓ Prepares procedural correspondence outlines (if safe)
  • ✓ Refuses output when attorney required
  • ✓ Provides structured, factual templates

⚠️ What This System WILL NOT DO

  • ✗ Negotiate with insurers
  • ✗ Argue coverage or policy interpretation
  • ✗ Respond to fraud investigations or EUO requests
  • ✗ Replace professional legal representation
  • ✗ Generate output when attorney involvement detected

When the System Will Refuse to Help

⛔ HARD STOP SCENARIOS

This system will intentionally refuse to generate output in the following scenarios. You MUST consult an attorney:

Fraud Investigations

Any letter mentioning fraud, misrepresentation, or false statements requires immediate attorney consultation.

Examination Under Oath (EUO)

EUO requests require attorney representation. Do not attend without legal counsel.

Reservation of Rights Letters

These letters question your policy coverage. Attorney consultation is mandatory.

Attorney Involvement

Any letter mentioning attorneys, legal counsel, or litigation requires professional representation.

Bad Faith Allegations

Bad faith claims involve complex legal issues requiring attorney expertise.

High-Value Commercial Claims

Commercial claims over $25,000 require professional representation.

When a hard stop is triggered, the system will explain why it cannot assist and recommend professional representation. This is a safety feature, not a limitation.

Types of Insurance Claim Letters We Process

Denial Letters

System prepares procedural acknowledgment and clarification request for claim denials (if no hard stop conditions detected).

Information Requests

System prepares procedural compliance response listing only requested documents (no narrative).

Partial Payment Letters

System prepares valuation dispute notification with factual discrepancies only (no arguments).

Appeal Opportunities

System prepares formal appeal submission with factual grounds (no legal arguments or persuasion).

⚠️ System output is limited to procedural correspondence only. No negotiation, no strategy, no legal advice.
